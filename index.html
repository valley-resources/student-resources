VCS Student Resources

Finances

Finances cover tuition and other costs associated with attending school. Understanding your financial options can help you plan and manage expenses effectively.

  • College Savings Tips: Strategies to help families save for college expenses.
  • FAFSA: The Free Application for Federal Student Aid is required by many colleges in order to offer any financial assistance, including scholarships. Even if you think that you will not qualify for financial aid, make time to fill out this application once you know where you will attend college. The sooner it is submitted, the more aid that is available.
  • Financial Aid Resources: The National Association of Student Financial Aid Administrators (NASFAA) answers common questions in applying for financial aid.

Scholarships are an excellent way to help pay for college. Many organizations offer scholarships based on various criteria, including academic achievement, community service, and personal background. Here is a list to get you started!

Choosing a College

Choosing a college is a significant decision that can shape your future. Consider factors such as location, size, programs offered, and campus culture when making your choice.

  • College Search Guide: This guide asks some important questions to help students decide what kinds of colleges are the best fit for them.

Career Exploration

High school is a great time for students to investigate and explore career paths for their future. There are a number of online surveys linked below to match a studentâ€™s interests and skills to possible career clusters. With some of those possibilities in mind, students are encouraged to look for opportunities to shadow others in those careers, attend exploration events, and pray about which pathway God is opening for them.

  • Career Surveys
    • O*NET Interest Profiler at My Next Move A short assessment to help students identify career paths that may interest them.
    • Pathway Planit A tool for career planning, exploration, and investigation.
    • Interest Assessment A tool to gauge student's interest in a profession.
    • Skills Matcher A tool to match a student's skillset to a career field.
    • Work Values Matcher A tool to match a student's values to a job.

  • Future You: Each fall we take our 8th graders to this career expo sponsored by the Oshkosh Area Chamber of Commerce. Designed specifically for their age group, it features area employers and possible career paths to help them dream about their future selves.

  • Career Exploration Events: This document is a work in progress, listing local companies and organizations that provide career exploration days. Many events include hands-on, interactive activities and presentations to help students understand the skills and training required for different careers.

  • Youth Apprenticeship Program: This program is sponsored by the Wisconsin Department of Workforce Development and coordinated by CESA 6, linking high school juniors and seniors with employers in desired career paths. YA provides opportunities to acquire valuable skills and to help determine if that career path is right.
